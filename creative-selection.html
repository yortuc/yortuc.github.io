Creative Selection [Book]

The full title of the book is Creative Selection: Inside Apple's Design Process During the Golden Age of Steve Jobs. Amazon^

started : 7-May-2020

finished: ~

  • Book starts with a memory of the writer with Steve Jobs, the demo is about IPad keyboard. He prepares two types of keyboard one is small keys but looks like the same with a physical keyboard and the other one is with bigger keys for comfort. ANd they they came up with an idea to switch btw keyboards with a nice animation. After Steve saw this, he asks: "We will go with one of this right?". This is a good point about product design. Take the decisions on behalf of the user. They cannot know what is better for them.
  • Then a memory about the secret development of Safari browser for mac desktor. How they initiated the initiative with 2 engineers only. And used free software (Konqueror) to start with the development
  • It was really interesting to learn how the iPhone team struggled to come up with a usable sfotware keyboard. We take it granted today, but, back at the day no one had any idea to type on a touchscreen.
  • Repetitions make the book little bit boring sometimes.
  • Also, you witness how Apple product design is focused on tiny details while reading the iPhone keyboard story. Apple has some unique dictatorship when it comes downs to take decisions on behalf of the user. Apply says, for the user, this way is better. And this works really well. At the end, you cannot ask to people if you want real innovation. As Henry Ford sai, if I had asked to people, they whould have wanted faster horses.
